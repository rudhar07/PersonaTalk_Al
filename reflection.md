Prompt engineering changed how I think about product quality. Initially, I assumed "good model + decent UI" would be enough for a persona chatbot. In practice, the model only became believable after the prompts were treated as product architecture rather than random instructions. The biggest lesson was that style does not come from one sentence like "be concise"; it comes from layered context, carefully designed examples, and explicit output controls.

What worked best was separating the three personas by worldview, not just word choice. For Anshuman, I enforced direct accountability and tactical execution. For Abhimanyu, I imposed structured diagnosis and measurable planning. For Kshitij, I emphasized intuitive teaching and confidence-building analogies. These distinctions made outputs feel materially different even for similar user questions. Few-shot examples were the strongest lever: they reduced generic responses and helped the model maintain consistency across turns.

The GIGO principle became very obvious during testing. Whenever the system prompt was vague, the responses were vague. Whenever I provided richer persona context and clearer constraints, the responses immediately improved in both tone and usefulness. Poor prompt input produced shallow output every single time, even with a strong underlying model. This validated that prompt quality is not optional in LLM applications; it is core application logic.

On the engineering side, streaming improved perceived intelligence because users saw responses build in real time, and persona switching with conversation reset prevented cross-persona contamination. Error handling was also important; without a graceful fallback message, the experience feels broken during key failures.

If I had more time, I would add an evaluation harness that scores persona fidelity and actionability across a fixed test set. I would also add conversation export and a "persona confidence" indicator that explains why a response fits the selected persona. Overall, this project showed that strong prompt design, clear UX states, and safe backend patterns together define a high-quality AI product.
